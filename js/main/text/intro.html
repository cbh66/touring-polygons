The Parts Cutting Problem Overview

We have a large sheet of plastic, and a laser head that can move across it. We want to cut out shapes from the plastic, and we want to do it fast: we want the laser head to travel the minimum possible distance to get to each of the polygons and cut them out.

The Parts Cutting Problem is a specific form of the Touring Polygons Problem, in which a path must be constructed to visit a sequence of polygons in the minimum distance under various constraints. These problems are described by the paper "Touring a Sequence of Polygons" by Moshe Dror, Alon Efrat, Anna Lubiw, and Joseph S. B. Mitchell. There are three important restrictions in this problem.

  • The order in which the polygons should be visited must be part of the input - otherwise the problem is as hard as the NP-Hard Travelling Salesperson Problem.
  • The polygons should not intersect, though they can contain one another. This has more to do with the definition of parts-cutting; the general solution to the Touring Polygons Problem permits intersection, but requires finding the intersection points and taking them into account.
  • The polygons must be convex. The authors of the paper show, by a clever reduction from 3SAT, that the problem is NP-Hard for arbitrary nonconvex polygons.
